Clue-Less!

OBJECTIVE

Mr. Boddy — apparently the victim of foul play — is found in one of the rooms of his mansion. To win, you must determine the answers to these three questions: Who done it? Where? And with What Weapon?

SETUP

At the beginning of a game of Clue-Less, all connected players will be prompted to select their character. In traditional Clue, Ms. Scarlett would always go first, but for fairness the first player to move will be selected at random. Once all of the connected players have selected their character, each player will be dealt three cards (one Suspect, one Room, and one Weapon), and the game will begin.

GAME PLAY

Moving Your Token

On each turn, try to reach a different room of the mansion. Every player’s first move must be to a Hallway that is adjacent to their starting Room. A player in a Room may move to any adjacent Hallway that is not already occupied by another player, or if a player is currently in a Corner Room, they may choose to use a Secret Passage. If you were moved to the current Room by another player making a Suggestion, you may, if you wish, stay in that room and make a Suggestion rather than moving. If all adjacent Hallways are blocked and you are not currently in a Corner Room, you may not move this turn, but you may make an Accusation if you so choose. A player which begins their turn in a Hallway must move one of the Rooms connected by that hallway and make a Suggestion.

Secret Passages

The rooms in opposite corners of the mansion are connected by Secret Passages. If you’re in one of these rooms at the start of your turn, you may, if you wish, use a Secret Passage instead of moving to a Hallway. Moving through a Secret Passage will place your token in the Room located in the opposite corner of the board.

 

Making a Suggestion

As soon as you enter a room, you will be prompted to make a Suggestion. By making Suggestions throughout the game, you try to determine— by process of elimination— which three cards are in the confidential Case File envelope. To make a Suggestion, select a Suspect and a Weapon, both of which will be moved to the Room you just entered. You have now made the Suggestion that the crime was committed in that Room, by that Suspect, with that Weapon. Example: Let’s say that you’re Miss Scarlet and you enter the Lounge. You will then be prompted to select a Suspect, Mr. Green, for instance, and a Weapon, the Wrench perhaps. When you’re satisfied with your Suggestion, you will click Confirm. Both the Suspect and the Weapon will then me moved to the Room which you are currently occupying, and all players will be shown “Player 1 has suggested the crime was committed in the Lounge by Mr. Green with the Wrench.”

Remember two things:

·         You must be in the Room that you mention in your Suggestion.

·         Be sure to consider all tokens— including spare Suspects and including yourself!— as falling under equal suspicion.

Proving a Suggestion True or False

As soon as you make a Suggestion, your opponents, in turn, try to prove it false. The first to try is the player below you on the Player’s List. This player looks at his or her cards to see if one of the three cards you just named is there. If the player does have one of the cards named, that card will be shown to you and no one else. If the player has more than one of the cards named, he or she will select just one to show you. If that opponent has none of the cards that you named, then the chance to prove your Suggestion false passes, in turn, to the next player in the rotation. As soon as one opponent shows you one of the cards that you named, it is proof that this card cannot be in the envelope. End your turn by checking off this card in your notebook. (Some players find it helpful to mark the initials of the player who showed the card.) If no one is able to prove your Suggestion false, you may either end your turn or make an Accusation now.

Making an Accusation

When you think you’ve figured out which three cards are in the envelope, you may, on your turn, make an Accusation and select any three elements you want. I.E., “I accuse (Suspect) of committing the crime in the (Room) with the (Weapon).” You will then be shown the three cards that are in the Confidential Case File envelope. In a Suggestion, the Room you name must be the Room where your token is located. But in an Accusation, you may name any room. Remember: You may make only one Accusation during a game.

If Your Accusation Is Incorrect

If any one of the cards that you named is not inside the Case File:

·         All three cards return to the Confidential Case File envelope.

·         You may make no further moves in the game, and therefore cannot win, but you do remain involved in the investigation.

·         You do continue to try to prove your opponents’ Suggestions false.

·         Your token will still be moved into the various Rooms when other players make Suggestions.

Winning

You win the game if your Accusation is completely correct— that is, if you find in the Confidential Case File envelope all three of the cards that you named. When this happens, all players are shown the cards and the game has ended.

Special Notes About Suggestions:

1.      When you make a Suggestion, you may, if you wish, name one or more of the cards that you hold in your own hand. You might want to do this to gain information or to mislead your opponents.

2.      You may, if you wish, make a Suggestion and an Accusation on the same turn.

3.      You may make only one Suggestion after entering a particular room. To make your next Suggestion, you must either enter a different room or, sometime after your next turn, re-enter the room that you most recently left. You may not forfeit a turn to remain in a particular room. But if you’re trapped in a room because your opponents are blocking the exits, you must remain there until an exit is unblocked and you can move out of the Room.

4.      You may make a Suggestion that includes a Suspect or Weapon that’s already in your Room.

5.      If yours was the Suspect transferred, you may, on your next turn, do one of two things:

Move from the Room in one of the usual ways OR Make a Suggestion for that room.

If you decide to make a Suggestion, you do not move that turn.

6.      There is no limit to the number of Suspects or Weapons that may be in one Room at one time.
